More Information

This part will disclose the domain registrar (the entity that you rent your domain from), your hosting provider (the entity that houses the files to your website), and the overall process that it takes to build a website for you.

First and foremost, I use Namecheap for my domain registrar. They constantly have excelent deals on domains and their customer service is supurb.

The chosen hosting provider depends on what kind of site you are wanting. For basic, static sites, I go with Github Pages. Their hosting is free, although it is a bit limiting. For more complex sites like blogs, I use Digital Ocean. Hosting is typically $5/month and allows a lot more flexibility.

To help ensure that your site is incredibly fast, I use Cloudflare for DNS.

The Development Process

First, you will fill out the survey listed below. This gives me some necessary information so that we are on the same page when I reach out to you. Once I review all of the details I will reach out to you so that we can further discuss the paramaters for your website. Once all of the necessary preperations are made, I ask that I receive a 30% 'down payment' so that, in the unlikly event that I cannot be paid in the end, all of my hard work won't be all for nothing.

Payments are made through Google Pay for ease of use. You will receive an email with the proper ammount. Once I recieve the payment for the downpayment, we will continue the development process.

We will continue to converce as I send you updates and you reply with suggestions. If all of the right pieces fall into place, you will have a brand new website and I will receive the remainder of my pay.
